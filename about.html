About Bagan

Bagan, also known as Pagan, is an ancient city located in the Mandalay Region of Myanmar (Burma). It is famous for its vast plain filled with thousands of Buddhist temples, pagodas, and monasteries. Once the capital of the Pagan Kingdom, Bagan flourished between the 11th and 13th centuries and is considered one of the greatest archaeological sites in Southeast Asia.

Bagan's iconic temples, such as Ananda Temple and Shwezigon Pagoda, attract millions of tourists each year. The stunning landscape, combined with the rich history and culture, makes it one of the most remarkable destinations for history and architecture enthusiasts.

History of Bagan

Bagan was the capital of the Pagan Kingdom, which existed from the 9th to the 13th centuries. The kingdom was one of the most powerful in Southeast Asia, and it played a pivotal role in the spread of Theravada Buddhism across the region.

During this period, more than 10,000 temples and pagodas were constructed in Bagan. These structures were built to honor the Buddha and to demonstrate the grandeur of the Pagan kings. Many of these ancient temples remain today as stunning examples of Burmese architecture and art.

Top Attractions in Bagan

Ananda Temple

Ananda Temple is one of the most famous temples in Bagan. Its unique architecture and stunning frescoes make it a must-see attraction.

Shwezigon Pagoda

The Shwezigon Pagoda is one of the most important religious sites in Bagan and is known for its golden stupa.

Bagan Sunset

The sunset in Bagan is one of the most picturesque experiences you can have. Watch the sun dip below the horizon, casting a golden glow over the temples.
